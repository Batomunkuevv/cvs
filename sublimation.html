What is Sublimation

Sublimation, in physics, is conversion of a substance from the solid to the gaseous state without its becoming liquid. The process of drying the products by first freezing them and them removing the ice under vacuum (sublimation of ice) is often called lyophilization, cryodesiccation or freeze drying

During the sublimation process, the material is cooled and vacuumed to bring it`s liquid (water) below the “triple state point” – the point at which solid, liquid and gas phases of the material coexist. The sublimation process should pass at lower than triple point temperature and pressure. The lower temperature and the pressure – the better dried product´s quality, but the longer the processing time. To find the “golden middle” is the well-kept secret of sublimation technology experts, like us.

However, we prefer to use the term “sublimation”, because we pay attention to the fact that our machines use only sublimation process to dry the product, while many conventional freeze dryers are also using heat to speed up the process (and worsen the product quality)

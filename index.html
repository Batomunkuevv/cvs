Company

Cryogenic and vacuum systems Ltd (CVS) was established in 2014 by experienced
scientists and engineers from aerospace industry with the purpose of bringing practical and reliable vacuum and cryogenic technologies to the consumer at an affordable price.

  • CVS is a member of the Latvian Chamber of Commerce and Industry, the Latvian Refrigeration Engineers Association, the Swedish Vacuum Society, the R&D partner of choice of many European scientific institutions.

  • CVS research work is a vital part of many European Comission, ESA, ERDF and Latvian funded applied research programs.

    -In 2017 CVS was awarded the Horizont 2020 SME phase I prize.

    -In 2018 European Comission awarded CVS by the Seal of Excellence.

  • In 2016, based on our detailed understanding of vacuum and cryogenic technologies, we invested in developing and commercializing our patented sublimation technology and started producing and selling our “DryEat(R)” sublimators.

What is Sublimation

Sublimation, in physics, is conversion of a substance from
solid to gaseous state without its’ becoming liquid. The process of drying the products by first freezing them and them removing the ice under vacuum (sublimation of ice) is also often called lyophilization, cryodesiccation or freeze drying

Preserving the harvest

Strawberries are a delicate product subject to vagaries of the weather. Once the weather is warm and dry, a lot of strawberries ripen in the fields, and the prices drop. One can choose not to sell at the dumping price to the supermarkets, but rather to preserve the harvest by sublimation. Good quality strawberries make delicious sublimated strawberries, which command retail prices of 120 Eur per kg and more.
